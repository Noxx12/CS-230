# Portfolio
Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?

The Gaming Room was the client, and they requested the design of a software application called "Draw It or Lose It." The software is a multiplayer game that allows users to draw a given prompt and compete against other players to guess what the prompt is.

What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?

In developing this documentation, I focused on providing clear and concise explanations for each section, including the recommended operating platform, storage management system, memory management techniques, distributed systems and networks, and security measures. I also made sure to consider the client's specific requirements and needs throughout the design process.

What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?

The process of working through the design document was helpful in identifying potential challenges and ensuring that all necessary components were included in the final software design.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

Interpreting the user's needs and implementing them into the software design was a key aspect of the project. It is important to consider the user's needs because ultimately, they will be the ones using the software, and their satisfaction and experience will determine the success of the application.

How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?

To approach designing software in the future, I would start by gathering a comprehensive list of requirements from the client and potential users. From there, I would analyze the necessary components and consider various design options and potential challenges before finalizing the software design. I would also prioritize testing and user feedback throughout the development process to ensure that the final product meets the needs and expectations of its users.
